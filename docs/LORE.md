# Rust Legions — The Lore Bible (v2)

> **Authority.** Canonical lore of Rust Legions. **v2 amends v1 per maintainer canon directive
> (2026-07-16):** humanity was the ward of a vast interstellar empire which, having pilfered the
> planet, suddenly left — leaving only scraps and ruins. Humanity stands at dieselpunk tech, has not
> yet reached for the stars, believes it one day might, and the great houses fight to find **the
> Key** to leaving their dead world. `docs/VISION.md` §2 remains the compressed summary; where this
> file elaborates, this file governs. Proposals graduate here only via `docs/LORE_TODO.md`.

## 0. Rules for Writing in This World

1. **What is known, and what is never answered.** It is canonical fact that the Empire existed, that
   humanity lived under its wardship, that it stripped this world of its wealth, and that it left
   *suddenly*. What no document, character, or system may ever canonically answer: **why the
   departure was sudden**, why humanity was wardened at all, the Empire's true name or present fate,
   and whether anything is ever coming back. All deep history remains *attributed, unreliable
   fragments* — contradiction between sources is a feature.
2. **Two registers.** Cold official designation and worn folk name, always. *Object 9 / "the Choir."
   Rot-count / "the Rent." Excavation Site 112 / "Grandmother's Door."*
3. **Hope is industrial-grade.** No prophecy that works, no magic. If humanity reaches the stars it
   will be riveted, fueled, and paid for. The sky is an engineering problem wearing a religion.
4. **Color is rationed.** Ash-grey world; red is signal, blood, and rust. Name a color only when it
   matters.
5. **Nothing large sits still.** Any big permanent city must be dead, dying, or an exception with an
   explanation (§4).

## 1. The Ground (a pilfered world)

The world has no agreed name. The ministries call it **the Ground**; the oldest imperial fragments —
and this is the detail that keeps scholars up at night — call it **the Site**. Not a home, not a
colony: a *work-site designation*. It was rich once. The Empire took the richness up and away for
longer than memory holds, and what the great houses fight over now are the dregs: thin seams, lean
lodes, and the ruins and scraps of the machines that did the taking. It is, by any honest ledger, a
dead world — habitable, stubborn, but spent. Which is why every house that matters is looking for
the way *off* it.

## 2. The Empire and the Four Departures

Humanity did not evolve here and did not come here free. It came — or was brought — as the **ward**
of a vast interstellar empire: fed, ordered, put to purpose, and never told anything. The Ground's
names for it are its own: **the Wardens** (the ministries' term), **the Landlords** (settler slang —
they collected, didn't they?), **the Absent** (the careful neutral usage). And then, on a day no
calendar preserved, the Empire *left* — not wound down, not withdrawn in stages: **left**, suddenly,
completely, taking its fleets and its answers and abandoning its wards amid the ruins of the
extraction.

Why the suddenness? That is the question under every war on the Ground. Four readings dominate:

| Departure | The claim | What it implies | Who holds it |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **The Recall** | The Empire was summoned home to some crisis — and means to return. The wardship is suspended, not ended. | Be ready. Preserve the works, learn the ways, light the signal. The Key is an *appeal*. | Restorationist orthodoxy; the Synod; the Procession; the parishes. |
| **The Finished Ledger** | The planet stopped paying. The job was done; the crews went home. Nothing personal. | No one is coming. Passage off this rock will be built or bought, never granted. | The Combine; the Emberwrights; every settlement that keeps books. |
| **The Flight** | They did not withdraw — they *fled*. Something below, or something out there, and the ruins are what got left in a panic. | The Key must never be turned. Whatever they ran from has our address. | The Covenant of Locks; the Quiet Parish; every outrider who won't dig. |
| **The Discarding** | "Ward" is the flattering word. Humanity was *equipment* — site labor — discarded with the rest of the gear when the Site closed. | We owe them nothing and ask them nothing. We climb on our own hull. | The hard-Reclaimer position; the Reclamation; much of the Commonweal. |

The **Creed axis** is this argument made mechanical: the Restorationist pole holds that the way up
runs *through* the Empire's works (reawaken, retrieve, or be retrieved); the Reclaimer pole holds
that humanity builds its own ascent and owes the Landlords not even curiosity. The Flight is the
axis's dark orthogonal — the creed of those who would break the Key rather than turn it.

## 3. History, in Fragments

### 3.1 The Reckoning (maintainer canon: the calendar and the two moons)

The Ground's day runs **36 hours** — a long light and a longer dark, lived in watches. Ten days make
a **ten-day**; four ten-days make a **month**; ten months — **400 days** — make one solar revolution
of the Ground. Years are counted **F.I.**, *since First Ignition*; the present is **383 F.I.**
Ministries date documents *Day N of the Xth Month, 383 F.I.*; armies keep their own count besides:
*"Day 214 of the March."* Months are numbered officially (the First Month through the Tenth); folk
names attach regionally and inconsistently, which is itself canon — write "the month the parishes
call the Thaw" and never a master list.

The reckoning is a scar. The solar year is the Ground's own, but the **ten-day is the Empire's
quota cycle**, kept alive out of habit older than memory: under the Wardship, the manifests were
posted and the quotas counted every tenth day — **lift-day** — and on the last lift-day no manifest
came. Humanity still measures its weeks in the Landlords' shift schedule, and the ministries have
never found a reason, or the nerve, to change it.

Two moons ride the long nights: the **First Light**, called **the Lamp** — broad, slow, and pale —
and the **Second Light**, called **the Coal** — small, fast, and dark, an ash-black stone that
occults stars as it passes. Bright nights under the full Lamp; **dark-runs** when only the Coal is
up, beloved of raiders, runners, and everyone the Lamp would hang. When the Coal crosses the Lamp's
face the parishes hold vigil, and even the ministries post no convoys. *"Between the Lamp and the
Coal"* — settler idiom for any hour when nothing is certain.

**The Wardship** *(duration unknown)* — humanity under the Empire: fed, counted, put to work at the
extraction. Almost nothing survives of it firsthand; the wards were not given records, or the
records went up with everything else. What remains is in the bones: every human language on the
Ground shares the same words for *quota*, *manifest*, and *lift-day*.

> *"…and on the last lift-day no manifest was posted, and the sky-cradles stood open, and no one
> came to count us."* — fragment, provenance disputed, kept under glass at the Nine Cradles

**The Withdrawal** *(date lost; the founding trauma)* — the sudden departure. Days or hours, not
years, by every account that claims to be an account. The Empire's leaving broke more than it left:
orbital works dropped, sites sealed mid-shift, wards abandoned amid machinery they had operated but
never understood.

**The Quiet Centuries** — scattered survival among the ruins; memory eroding into hymn and grudge.

**The Ignition — 0 F.I.** — a fuel seam struck, and in the same generation the first sealed cache
pried open. Diesel civilization is born — not invented but *scavenged*: copied badly from imperial
scrap. This is why the Ground fields thousand-ton land-fortresses and no thinking machines, and no
wings that reach past the weather: humanity reproduced what it could pry open, and the Empire did
not leave its starships behind. A single *intact* relic is a page of the original book.

**The Cartel Wars — c. 90–141 F.I.** — the seam-cartels fight over the dregs. Fixed cities rise on
the richest ruins — and sicken (§4).

**The First March — 141 F.I.** — the Siege of the Hundredweight: an encircled mining combine
unbolts its refinery, mounts it on salvaged treads, and drives out through the lines — the **First
Keel**. Within a generation the age of fortress-bases, the **Long March era**, has begun.

**The Present — 383 F.I.** — the great houses roam a spent world, racing one another through its
ruins for the scraps that might, assembled, amount to **the Key**: the way off. Nobody agrees what
the Key is — a signal, a ship, a door, an appeal — but every house believes it will be the one to
find it, and that finding it will justify everything done on the road there.

## 4. Ground-Rot, or: Why Nothing Large Sits Still

The Ground keeps no cities. Settle too many people too densely too long — above all on worked
ground, which is most ground worth having — and the **rot** sets in: wasting, failed harvests,
stillbirths, and the dreams the diggers call *machine-sleep*. Ministries measure **rot-counts**;
parishes call it **the Rent** and say the Landlords still collect. Whether it is the wound of the
extraction, the exhaust of what lies buried, or something older is — per §0.1 — never answered.
What it forces is the shape of the world: settlements stay small, the great powers stay moving, and
the still-cities of the Cartel Wars are ruins with very good bones. A living still-city is rare,
walled, and lying about something.

## 5. The Leavings (precursor relics)

What the Empire abandoned, the Ground calls **the leavings**; the ministries assign **Object
numbers**; the folk assign names within the week. Four classes, per the captured survey typology:

| Class | What it is | In play |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Caches** | Sealed stores — alloys, fuel, components | One-off windfalls; the "break it for parts" temptation |
| **Engines** | Functional imperial machinery | The unique base modules — found, never built |
| **Ciphers** | Archives, cores, marked stones — *information* | Progress toward the Key; decree triggers; the Synod's obsession |
| **Wakes** | Machinery still running | Hazards: wardens that stir, beacons that answer to no one on this world |

Digger culture: a **red flag** flies over every unclassified find — the one signal every house
honors, because a misread Wake buries all creeds equally. And every digger knows the whispered
arithmetic: the Ciphers are pages, the Engines are parts, and somewhere in the sum of them is a way
past the sky.

## 6. The Settled

Settlements are polities of their ground and nothing beyond it; they trade with, submit to, or are
raided by whichever fortress rolls past — and they remember. Types per the roster: burn-towns,
mining combines, farm communes, waystations, scrap-parishes, still-cities. The **Anchor Fields** —
the vast cradle-ruins where the Empire's lifting structures stood, where the world's wealth went
*up* — are the holiest and most contested ground of all: pilgrimage sites to those awaiting the
Recall, quarries to those balancing the Finished Ledger, and sealed perimeters wherever the Covenant
can enforce one.

## 7. The Great Houses

Ten houses (see `FACTION_ROSTER.md`), every one an answer to the Departure and a bid for the Key:
the **Iron Reclamation** would unify humanity and build its own hull (the Discarding, armed); the
**Charter Combine** intends to own the ticket booth whenever the way up opens (the Ledger,
incorporated); the **Bastion Synod** preserves the means until humanity is worthy of the Recall;
the **Covenant of Locks** exists so that no one turns the Key at all (the Flight, militant); the
**Signal Ascendancy** broadcasts to the Absent, demanding an answer or an audition; the
**Commonweal March** holds that a people who cannot feed each other have no business among stars;
the **Salvage Court** adjudicates everyone else's salvage at knifepoint; the **Emberwright Union**
means to beat the Rent and build the New Ignition — a human foundry aimed at the sky; the **Long
Procession** crusades to gather the leavings into worthy hands; the **Outrider Compact** keeps the
doors shut, the dead paid, and the intelligence priced fairly.

## 8. Keels

Every fortress-base traces (or forges) lineage to the First Keel of 141 F.I. Full style: *the*
**[Name]**, *[Ordinal] [Keel-term] of [House]*. Names are **vows, debts, or verdicts** — abstract
noun bound to material: *Vow of Coal. Debt of Winters. Verdict of Iron. Patience of Rust.*

## 9. Glossary

**the Ground / the Site** — the world, in ministry and imperial usage · **the Empire / Wardens /
Landlords / the Absent** — the departed power · **the Wardship** — the era under them · **the
Withdrawal** — the sudden leaving · **lift-day / manifest / quota** — Wardship-era words in every
tongue · **the Key** — the assembled way off-world; the headline prize · **the leavings** — relic
material · **F.I.** — years since First Ignition (now 383); a year is 10 months of 4 ten-days — 400 days of 36 hours · **ten-day** — the ten-day week, the Empire's quota cycle kept alive · **the Lamp / the Coal** — the two moons (First and Second Lights); a **dark-run** is a Coal-only night · **keel** — a fortress-base and its
lineage · **the March** — a house's campaign · **the Rent / rot-count** — ground-rot, folk and
official · **Anchor Field** — cradle-ruin of the Empire's lifting works · **Object [N]** — relic
designation, always paired with a folk name · **red flag** — unclassified find, don't touch ·
**machine-sleep** — the diggers' dreams · **red traffic** — combat-band radio · **the Quiet** — the
lost centuries; settler slang for death · **swath** — a keel's depleted trail.
